Conway's Game of Life

Conway's Game of Life is a cellular automaton devised by the British mathematician John Horton Conway in 1970. It features an infinitely large two-dimensional array consisting of cells which must be in either of two states: dead or alive. The rules for determining whether a cell lives or dies are dependent upon the surrounding cells. For example, in the standard game of life, a cell survives a generation only if it has exactly 2 or 3 living neighbors. A dead cell is brought to life only if it has exactly 3 living neighbors.

This simulation differs from the classic game of life in that it uses a toroidal array. That is, instead of having unlimited space to grow, cells are bound to finite dimensions based on the amount of space available on the screen.

Microblog

This is a quick prototype for a self-hosted, database-free static microblogging system. The ultimate goal is to create a federation of loosely coupled blog streams, for those of us who want to share quick thoughts, connect with people by sharing tiny public messages, or whatever else we want to do in ~256 characters. All of this I'd like to do in an extremely lightweight fashion such that anybody can host their own microblogging node with little to no experience.
